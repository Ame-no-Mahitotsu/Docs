## Context

Gabriele makes **individual nativity houses** — standalone buildings and structures (houses, stables, workshops, inns) that compose a presepio scene. He does **not** sell complete nativity sets (figures + stable + scene). This distinction is commercially and semantically important: the product is the architecture, not the scene.

Primary EN market: **United Kingdom**. Secondary: English-speaking international (US, AU, IE).

---

## Candidate Terms Evaluated

### 1. "nativity house" / "nativity houses"

**What it means to searchers:** A building in a nativity scene — closest match to Gabriele's product.

**Relative volume:** Low. "Nativity house" is not an established English compound noun. Most English speakers would not reach for this term unprompted — they would say "nativity stable" or "nativity scene building".

**Search intent match:** Very high — captures exactly what the product is.

**Competition:** Very low — few established players target this term, which means ranking is achievable but the audience is small.

**Verdict:** Accurate but under-searched. Useful as a secondary/long-tail target.

---

### 2. "nativity stable" / "nativity stable handmade"

**What it means to searchers:** The main structure in a nativity scene — the stable/manger where the central figures are placed. In English this is the default term for any nativity building.

**Relative volume:** Moderate–high. "Nativity stable" is the most searched English term for a nativity building. UK buyers looking for a handcrafted nativity structure will most naturally use this.

**Search intent match:** Good, with a caveat. Searchers using "nativity stable" are looking for a building — which is what Gabriele sells. The term technically refers to one specific building (the stable), while Gabriele's range includes other house types. However, in English usage "nativity stable" has broadened to cover any nativity scene structure, not just the stable itself.

**Competition:** Moderate. Mass-market retailers (Amazon, John Lewis, Etsy) dominate broad terms. Handmade + artisan qualifiers reduce the competitive field significantly.

**Verdict:** Best volume / intent balance. Should be the primary target term, qualified with "handmade" or "Italian".

---

### 3. "nativity building" / "nativity scene building"

**What it means to searchers:** A building component for a nativity display.

**Relative volume:** Very low. "Nativity building" is not a natural English phrase. "Nativity scene building" is slightly more natural but rarely searched.

**Search intent match:** Accurate but the phrasing is awkward in English.

**Verdict:** Do not target. Low volume, unnatural phrasing.

---

### 4. "nativity scene"

**What it means to searchers:** The complete nativity display — figures, stable, and scene together.

**Relative volume:** High. Broad term, heavily searched — especially in November/December.

**Search intent match:** Poor. Buyers searching "nativity scene" are looking for a complete set. Gabriele's product does not satisfy this intent and would produce high bounce rates.

**Verdict:** Do not target as primary. Appears naturally in copy but should not be the primary page keyword.

---

### 5. "handmade nativity" / "handcrafted nativity"

**What it means to searchers:** A nativity item (stable, scene, or figures) made by hand — artisan quality.

**Relative volume:** Low–moderate. Niche but high-intent. Buyers using these terms are specifically seeking artisan quality and are less price-sensitive — strong match for Gabriele's customer profile.

**Search intent match:** High. The artisan dimension is a key differentiator for Gabriele.

**Verdict:** Strong secondary term. Works best as a qualifier: "handmade nativity stable", "handcrafted nativity house".

---

### 6. "Italian nativity" / "Italian nativity scene"

**What it means to searchers:** A nativity item made in Italy — Italian craftsmanship carries prestige in this category (Naples presepe tradition is internationally known).

**Relative volume:** Low–moderate. "Italian nativity scene" has some recognition; "Italian nativity stable" is niche.

**Search intent match:** High quality-signal. Buyers searching "Italian nativity" associate it with quality craftsmanship — aligns with Gabriele's positioning.

**Verdict:** Strong secondary term and brand signal. Works as a modifier. Does not need to be a standalone page target.

---

### 7. "presepe" / "presepio" (Italian terms in EN context)

**What it means to searchers:** The Italian-specific term for nativity scene, used by enthusiasts, collectors, and diaspora buyers.

**Relative volume:** Very low in English. Recognised by a narrow but highly engaged audience (Italian heritage communities, serious collectors, specialist retailers).

**Search intent match:** Excellent for the right niche — someone searching "presepe" in English knows exactly what they want.

**Verdict:** Include in page copy and metadata for long-tail coverage, but do not make it a primary target. Its value is in signalling authenticity to discerning buyers, not in driving volume.

---

## Recommendation

### Primary target term: **"handmade nativity stable"**

Rationale:
- "Nativity stable" is the highest-volume English term for a nativity building
- "Handmade" qualifier aligns with Gabriele's product, reduces mass-market competition, and attracts the right buyer intent
- The term is understood across all EN markets (UK, US, IE, AU)
- Acceptable semantic fit: English speakers use "nativity stable" broadly for nativity scene buildings, not just the stable structure specifically

### Secondary terms (use in copy and metadata):
- **"nativity houses"** — accurate product descriptor; use in h1 and product descriptions
- **"handcrafted nativity"** — brand signal; use in introductory copy
- **"Italian nativity"** — provenance signal; use in About / Chi Sono page crosslinks
- **"presepio"** / **"presepe"** — authenticity signal; include in copy naturally, not forced

### Do not target:
- "nativity scene" (wrong intent — implies full set)
- "nativity building" (unnatural English phrasing)

## Honesty Note

Search volume figures above are **relative estimates**, not data from live tools (Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, SEMrush). The relative ranking (high / moderate / low) is based on domain knowledge of EN search behaviour in the seasonal gifts and artisan crafts category. Before a paid campaign or significant SEO investment, these estimates should be validated against live keyword data. For organic SEO on a site of this scale and niche, the qualitative analysis above is sufficient to make a sound copy and metadata decision.
